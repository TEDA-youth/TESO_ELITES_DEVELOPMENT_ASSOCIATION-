Introduction

These Terms and Conditions govern your membership and participation in Teso Elites Development Association (TEDA), a youth led organization operating in the Teso and Bukeddi sub regions of Eastern Uganda. By registering as a member or applying for any TEDA position, you agree to be bound by these terms.

Last updated: January 2026  |  Questions: tedayouthteso@gmail.com

Section 1: Membership

1 Eligibility

Membership of TEDA is open to all youth who share the vision of empowering the Teso sub region through education, culture, and sustainable development. There is no discrimination based on gender, religion, tribe, disability, or background. TEDA welcomes all youth committed to its mission and values.

2 Registration

All members must register through the official TEDA registration form providing accurate and truthful personal information. Providing false, misleading, or incomplete information is grounds for immediate termination of membership without notice.

3 Member Status

Upon registration, all accounts are set to pending and must be approved by TEDA administration before full membership is granted. TEDA reserves the right to approve or reject any membership application without obligation to provide reasons. Members will be notified of their approval status via email.

4 Subscription

Certain TEDA positions and activities may require members to be subscribed. Subscription details including fees and payment methods are communicated through official TEDA channels. Nonpayment of subscription where required may affect participation in specific programs or eligibility for certain positions.

5 Username & Password

Members are solely responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of their login credentials. Any activity carried out under your account is your responsibility. You must immediately notify TEDA administration of any unauthorized use of your account.

Section 2: Conduct & Values

6 Code of Conduct

All TEDA members are expected to uphold the organization's ten core values at all times: Unity, Integrity, Inclusivity, Respect, Accountability, Volunteerism, Innovation, Service to Community, Youth Empowerment, and Cultural Preservation.

7 Respectful Engagement

Members must treat fellow members, leaders, partners, and the public with dignity and respect in all interactions both online and offline. Harassment, discrimination, bullying, hate speech, or any form of abusive behavior will result in immediate suspension or permanent removal from TEDA.

8 Political Neutrality

TEDA is a strictly nonpartisan organization. Members must not use the TEDA platform, name, logo, or resources to advance personal political agendas or campaigns. TEDA leaders are required to be apolitical in their official capacity.

9 Confidentiality

Members must not share, publish, or misuse confidential information obtained through their involvement with TEDA. This includes member personal data, financial information, internal organizational matters, and strategic plans. This obligation continues even after membership ends.

10 Social Media & Online Conduct

Members representing TEDA on social media or any online platform must do so responsibly and in alignment with TEDA's values and mission. Content that brings TEDA into disrepute or spreads misinformation may result in disciplinary action.

Section 3: Leadership & Positions

11 Voluntary Service

All TEDA leadership positions are strictly voluntary. TEDA does not offer salaries, allowances, stipends, or any form of financial compensation to its leaders or members for their service to the organization.

12 Commitment to Service

Members appointed to leadership positions commit to serving for the agreed term of service without abandoning their responsibilities. Early withdrawal without reasonable cause may affect future consideration for positions.

13 Accountability of Leaders

All TEDA leaders are accountable to the membership and must conduct themselves with transparency and integrity. Misuse of the TEDA name, logo, resources, or position for personal or political gain is strictly prohibited.

14 Application for Positions

Applications for TEDA leadership and strategic positions must be submitted through official channels by the stated deadline. Late applications will not be considered under any circumstance.

15 Shortlisting & Selection

Only shortlisted candidates will be contacted following the application deadline. TEDA's selection decisions are final and binding. No correspondence will be entered into regarding unsuccessful applications.

Section 4: Data & Privacy

16 Data Collection

TEDA collects personal information including name, contact details, school, district, gender, and reason for joining for the purpose of membership management, program delivery, and organizational communication.

17 Data Sharing

TEDA will not sell, share, rent, or distribute member personal information to third parties without explicit written consent, except where required by applicable Ugandan law or authorized regulatory bodies.

18 Member Directory

By registering, members agree that their name and school or institution may be displayed in a public TEDA member directory to promote community visibility. Contact details are protected and only visible to logged in members where applicable.

Section 5: General

19 Amendments

TEDA reserves the right to update, amend, or revise these Terms and Conditions at any time without prior notice. Members will be notified of significant changes through official TEDA communication channels.

20 Termination

TEDA reserves the right to suspend or permanently remove any member who violates these Terms and Conditions or acts contrary to TEDA's mission and values. Members may also voluntarily withdraw at any time by contacting tedayouthteso@gmail.com.

Ready to Join TEDA?

By registering you confirm that you have read, understood, and agree to these Terms and Conditions and TEDA's core values.
