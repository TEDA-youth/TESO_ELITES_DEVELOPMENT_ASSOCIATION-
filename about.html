Who We Are

Teso Elites Development Association

Teso Elites Development Association (TEDA) is a youth led movement founded by the young people of the Teso sub region in Eastern Uganda. We exist to unite, empower, and equip youth to become leaders of education, culture, innovation, and sustainable development in our communities.

TEDA was established on the belief that the youth of Teso are not just beneficiaries of development, they are drivers of it. Through mentorship, dialogue, community engagement, and advocacy, we create opportunities for young people to unlock their full potential and contribute meaningfully to regional transformation.

TEDA is one of the largest youth led organizations operating in the Teso and Bukeddi sub regions of Uganda. Founded by passionate young people who understood firsthand the challenges facing their communities, TEDA was established to respond to the pressing social, economic, health, and environmental issues affecting young people and vulnerable families.

A Movement, Not Just an Organization

TEDA is more than an organization, it is a movement of young people determined to shape a brighter future for the Teso region. Every member, volunteer, and partner is part of something greater than themselves.

Our Direction

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

An empowered generation of Teso youth driving education, culture, and sustainable development for a thriving future that honors the past while building boldly toward tomorrow.

Our Mission

To empower the youth of Teso to lead in education, preserve culture, and champion sustainable development, creating opportunities for young people to unlock their full potential and contribute meaningfully to regional transformation.

Our Work

What We Do

At TEDA, we focus on practical and measurable impact across key areas.

Education & Mentorship

Supporting vulnerable students to reduce school dropouts, providing mentorship programs and leadership training, and linking youth to scholarship and skilling opportunities.

Youth Leadership & Civic Engagement

Training young leaders in governance and accountability, amplifying youth voices in district and national forums, and organizing dialogue platforms such as the annual Youth Forum.

Economic Empowerment

Connecting youth to vocational training, supporting entrepreneurship and innovation, and promoting self reliance and job creation rather than job seeking.

Health & Well being

Reproductive health awareness programs, community sensitization, malaria prevention awareness, and formation of school and community health clubs.

Cultural Preservation

Promoting unity, identity, and pride in Teso heritage, and strengthening intergenerational dialogue between youth and elders to preserve our culture.

Climate Action

Tree planting initiatives, green housing promotion, environmental awareness programs, and empowering young people to become climate actors and sustainable farmers.

Our Goals

Our Objectives

Concrete, measurable commitments that guide everything we do.

Reduce school dropout in Teso by supporting at least 500 vulnerable youth annually through mentorship, scholarships, and community sensitization.

Promote reproductive health education, with a special focus on girls, by conducting awareness campaigns and training at least 1,000 youth each year.

Strengthen youth leadership by training 200 young leaders annually in governance, cultural preservation, and community service.

Foster intergenerational dialogue by organizing at least 2 forums per year that bring together youth, elders, and leaders to promote peace, unity, and cultural values.

Support youth economic empowerment by linking at least 150 youth annually to vocational training, entrepreneurship programs, or start up opportunities.

Expand youth participation in decision making by ensuring representation of TEDA members in at least 3 district or national youth forums each year.

Promote youth led initiatives in education, culture, and social development by funding or supporting at least 10 projects annually.

What Guides Us

Our Core Values

Unity

We believe that strength lies in togetherness, and that Teso youth can achieve more when they work as one. Unity helps us overcome divisions and challenges by focusing on our shared goals.

Respect

Respect means honoring the dignity of every person, regardless of age, gender, or background. It includes valuing the wisdom of elders and the aspirations of the younger generation.

Inclusivity

TEDA creates space for all youth to participate, including those from marginalized or vulnerable backgrounds, ensuring no one is left behind.

Integrity

Integrity guides TEDA to act with honesty, transparency, and accountability in everything we do, earning the trust of members, partners, and community.

Volunteerism

We recognize that true change is driven by youth who give their time, energy, and skills selflessly, teaching responsibility and commitment to a greater cause.

Youth Empowerment

TEDA believes young people are capable of transforming society when given the right opportunities through education, mentorship, and leadership skills.

Cultural Preservation

We value the rich culture and traditions of Teso as a foundation for identity and pride, connecting youth to their roots while preparing them for the future.

Innovation

TEDA embraces creativity and new ideas as tools for solving community challenges, using technology, entrepreneurship, and modern skills.

Accountability

Accountability means taking responsibility for our actions, decisions, and commitments, upholding trust, discipline, and organizational credibility.

Service to Community

We exist to serve the needs of the Teso community through education, mentorship, health, and cultural initiatives, putting community before self.

How We Operate

Our Structure

TEDA operates under a formal constitution that provides clear governance and accountability. The organization is led by an elected Executive Committee supported by District (Zonal) Coordinators, Institutional Ambassadors, and Elders and external advisors.

We are a volunteer driven organization, no salaries are paid to leaders, ensuring that service and community impact remain at the heart of our mission.

Executive CommitteeElected leadership guiding the organization
District (Zonal) CoordinatorsManaging operations across districts
Institutional AmbassadorsRepresenting TEDA in institutions
Elders & External AdvisorsProviding wisdom and guidance

Our Flagship Initiative

One of our key platforms is the annual Youth Forum, which brings together young people, leaders, policymakers, and community stakeholders to dialogue on education, entrepreneurship, climate action, health, and accountable leadership.

This forum reflects our commitment to building bridges between youth and decision makers while generating practical solutions for sustainable development.
